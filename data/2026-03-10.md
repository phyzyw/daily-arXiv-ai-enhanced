### [1] [Scaling Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials with Mixtures of Experts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07977)
*Yuzhi Liu, Duo Zhang, Anyang Peng, Weinan E, Linfeng Zhang, Han Wang*

Main category: physics.chem-ph

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种基于混合专家（MoE）和混合线性专家（MoLE）的机器学习原子势（MLIP）架构，通过稀疏激活和元素级路由，显著提升了MLIP的表达能力和准确性，并在多个基准测试中取得了最先进的结果。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有MLIP模型在提升表达能力方面面临挑战，简单地扩大模型参数会导致计算成本过高和优化困难。MoE架构有望解决这些问题，实现更大规模的建模。

Method: 研究人员系统地开发了MoE和MoLE架构，分析了路由策略和专家设计的影响。他们采用了稀疏激活、共享专家、元素级路由和非线性专家特化等策略，并将其应用于OMol25、OMat24和OC20M基准数据集。

Result: 实验结果表明，稀疏激活和共享专家可以带来显著的性能提升，非线性MoE优于MoLE，元素级路由优于配置级路由，且模型在多个基准测试中实现了最先进的准确性。路由模式分析显示，专家特化与元素周期表趋势一致，表明模型能够有效捕捉元素特定的化学特性。

Conclusion: 本文提出的元素级MoE模型为构建高精度、可扩展的MLIP提供了一种有效途径，为材料科学、药物发现和催化设计等领域的研究提供了强大的工具。

Abstract: Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (MLIPs) enable accurate large-scale atomistic simulations, yet improving their expressive capacity efficiently remains challenging. Here we systematically develop Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) and Mixture-of-Linear-Experts (MoLE) architectures for MLIPs and analyze the effects of routing strategies and expert designs. We show that sparse activation combined with shared experts yields substantial performance gains, and that nonlinear MoE formulations outperform MoLE when shared experts are present, underscoring the importance of nonlinear expert specialization. Furthermore, element-wise routing consistently surpasses configuration-level routing, while global MoE routing often leads to numerical instability. The resulting element-wise MoE model achieves state-of-the-art accuracy across the OMol25, OMat24, and OC20M benchmarks. Analysis of routing patterns reveals chemically interpretable expert specialization aligned with periodic-table trends, indicating that the model effectively captures element-specific chemical characteristics for precise interatomic modeling.

### [2] [Integral Formulas for Vector Spherical Tensor Products](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08630)
*Valentin Heyraud, Zachary Weller-Davies, Jules Tilly*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文推导了向量球张量积的积分公式，并给出了闭合形式的表达式，从而简化了计算，并能用单个向量球张量积模拟Clebsch-Gordan张量积。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统的Clebsch-Gordan张量积计算成本高昂，Gaunt张量积虽然能加速计算，但无法重现反称量分。为了解决这个问题，Xie等人引入了向量球张量积(VSTP)，但其实现较为繁琐。本文旨在简化VSTP的实现，并探索其在SO(3)-等变神经网络中的应用。

Method: 本文基于Xie等人的工作，推导了向量球张量积的简单闭合形式积分表达式，并给出了反称量分对应的闭合形式表达式。通过将对称和反称量分结合到单个积分表示中，实现了用单个VSTP模拟Clebsch-Gordan张量积。

Result: 本文得到了向量球张量积的反称量分闭合形式积分表达式，并证明可以用单个VSTP模拟Clebsch-Gordan张量积，从而将张量积评估次数减少9倍。

Conclusion: 本文的成果简化了向量球张量积的实现，为SO(3)-等变神经网络中Gaunt张量积的推广应用铺平了道路，并为控制表达能力和运行时之间的权衡提供了新的视角。

Abstract: We derive integral formulas that simplify the Vector Spherical Tensor Product recently introduced by Xie et al., which generalizes the Gaunt tensor product to antisymmetric couplings. In particular, we obtain explicit closed-form expressions for the antisymmetric analogues of the Gaunt coefficients. This enables us to simulate the Clebsch-Gordan tensor product using a single Vector Spherical Tensor Product, yielding a $9\times$ reduction in the required tensor product evaluations. Our results enable efficient and practical implementations of the Vector Spherical Tensor Product, paving the way for applications of this generalization of Gaunt tensor products in $\mathrm{SO}(3)$-equivariant neural networks. Moreover, we discuss how the Gaunt and the Vector Spherical Tensor Products allow to control the expressivity-runtime tradeoff associated with the usual Clebsch-Gordan Tensor Products. Finally, we investigate low rank decompositions of the normalizations of the considered tensor products in view of their use in equivariant neural networks.

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 研究人员开发了一种 Physics-Informed Neural Operator (PINO) 架构的 AI 代理模型，将基本的物理原理嵌入到学习架构中，用于预测阈值电压 (Vth) 变化和保持行为。该模型通过损失函数强制执行控制方程，从而实现快速推断。

### [4] [Reverse Distillation: Consistently Scaling Protein Language Model Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07710)
*Darius Catrina, Christian Bepler, Samuel Sledzieski, Rohit Singh*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种名为Reverse Distillation的新框架，用于解决蛋白质语言模型(PLM)规模扩大后性能下降的问题。该框架将大型PLM的表示分解为由较小模型引导的正交子空间，确保大型模型始终优于小型模型。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 与自然语言处理和计算机视觉不同，蛋白质语言模型(PLM)的扩展规律不明确，甚至出现规模越大性能越差的现象。小型模型经常优于大型模型，且难以预测哪些生物任务会表现出不良的扩展行为。此外，不同规模的PLM嵌入表示彼此不连通。

Method: Reverse Distillation框架将大型PLM表示分解为正交子空间，由较小模型引导。这种方法创建了一种嵌套的Matryoshka-style结构，其中大型模型的前k维表示与较小模型的表示完全相同。通过隔离共享特征并从大型模型中提取额外的贡献，防止特征之间的干扰。

Result: 在ProteinGym基准测试中，Reverse Distillation的ESM-2变体在相同嵌入维度下优于其基线模型。150亿参数的Reverse Distillation模型实现了最强的性能。

Conclusion: Reverse Distillation框架为解决PLM扩展问题提供了一种通用的方法，并为生物基础模型的设计提供了新的思路。该框架可以推广到任何存在扩展挑战的模型系列，并为后续研究提供了代码和训练模型。

Abstract: Unlike the predictable scaling laws in natural language processing and computer vision, protein language models (PLMs) scale poorly: for many tasks, models within the same family plateau or even decrease in performance, with mid-sized models often outperforming the largest in the family. We introduce Reverse Distillation, a principled framework that decomposes large PLM representations into orthogonal subspaces guided by smaller models of the same family. The resulting embeddings have a nested, Matryoshka-style structure: the first k dimensions of a larger model's embedding are exactly the representation from the smaller model. This ensures that larger reverse-distilled models consistently outperform smaller ones. A motivating intuition is that smaller models, constrained by capacity, preferentially encode broadly-shared protein features. Reverse distillation isolates these shared features and orthogonally extracts additional contributions from larger models, preventing interference between the two. On ProteinGym benchmarks, reverse-distilled ESM-2 variants outperform their respective baselines at the same embedding dimensionality, with the reverse-distilled 15 billion parameter model achieving the strongest performance. Our framework is generalizable to any model family where scaling challenges persist. Code and trained models are available at https://github.com/rohitsinghlab/plm_reverse_distillation.

### [5] [Prediction of Steady-State Flow through Porous Media Using Machine Learning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06762)
*Jinhong Wang, Matei C. Ignuta-Ciuncanu, Ricardo F. Martinez-Botas, Teng Cao*

Main category: physics.flu-dyn

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种基于机器学习的框架，用于预测多孔介质中的稳态流动，并通过比较卷积自编码器、U-Net和傅里叶神经网络算子(FNO)三种模型，证明了FNO在速度和精度上优于传统CFD方法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统计算流体动力学(CFD)方法在优化冷板等热管理组件时，由于计算成本高昂，难以应用于复杂几何形状。因此，需要一种计算效率更高的替代方案。

Method: 研究人员开发了一种机器学习框架，用于预测由Navier-Stokes-Brinkman方程控制的多孔介质中的稳态流动。他们实现了并比较了三种模型架构：卷积自编码器(AE)、U-Net和傅里叶神经网络算子(FNO)，并结合了物理信息损失函数以提高物理一致性。

Result: 实验结果表明，FNO模型在预测精度上优于AE和U-Net，均方误差(MSE)低至0.0017，同时实现了高达1000倍的加速，并且具有网格无关性。

Conclusion: 该研究表明，机器学习在加速多孔介质中的流体流动预测方面具有潜力，为传统数值方法提供了一种可扩展的替代方案，特别适用于拓扑优化任务。

Abstract: Solving flow through porous media is a crucial step in the topology optimisation of cold plates, a key component in modern thermal management. Traditional computational fluid dynamics (CFD) methods, while accurate, are often prohibitively expensive for large and complex geometries. In contrast, data-driven surrogate models provide a computationally efficient alternative, enabling rapid and reliable predictions. In this study, we develop a machine-learning framework for predicting steady-state flow through porous media governed by the Navier-Stokes-Brinkman equations. We implement and compare three model architectures-convolutional autoencoder (AE), U-Net, and Fourier Neural Operator (FNO)-evaluating their predictive performance. To enhance physics consistency, we incorporate physics-informed loss functions. Our results demonstrate that FNO outperforms AE and U-Net, achieving a mean squared error (MSE) as low as 0.0017 while providing speedups of up to 1000 times compared to CFD. Additionally, the mesh-invariant property of FNO emphasizes its suitability for topology optimisation tasks, where varying mesh resolutions are required. This study highlights the potential of machine learning to accelerate fluid flow predictions in porous media, offering a scalable alternative to traditional numerical methods.

</details>
