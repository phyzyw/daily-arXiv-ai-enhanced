### [1] [Fast Bayesian equipment condition monitoring via simulation based inference: applications to heat exchanger health](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20735)
*Peter Collett, Alexander Johannes Stasik, Simone Casolo, Signe Riemer-Sørensen*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种基于模拟推断（SBI）的快速贝叶斯设备状态监测框架，利用神经网络估计后验分布，显著加速了推理速度，并能有效诊断换热器的故障。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统贝叶斯方法（如MCMC）在工业设备状态监测中面临计算瓶颈，难以实现实时过程控制。需要一种更快速、可扩展的替代方案来诊断复杂故障并实现数字孪生。

Method: 该框架利用模拟推断（SBI）和amortized神经网络后验估计，通过在模拟数据集上训练神经网络密度估计器，学习从热流体观测到降解参数后验分布的直接映射。

Result: SBI在各种合成污垢和泄漏场景下，与MCMC基线相比，实现了可比的诊断精度和可靠的不确定性量化，推理时间加速了82倍。

Conclusion: SBI作为一种高度可扩展的实时替代方案，为复杂工程系统的概率故障诊断和数字孪生实现提供了新的可能性，尤其适用于内部状态难以直接测量的工业设备。

Abstract: Accurate condition monitoring of industrial equipment requires inferring latent degradation parameters from indirect sensor measurements under uncertainty. While traditional Bayesian methods like Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) provide rigorous uncertainty quantification, their heavy computational bottlenecks render them impractical for real-time process control. To overcome this limitation, we propose an AI-driven framework utilizing Simulation-Based Inference (SBI) powered by amortized neural posterior estimation to diagnose complex failure modes in heat exchangers. By training neural density estimators on a simulated dataset, our approach learns a direct, likelihood-free mapping from thermal-fluid observations to the full posterior distribution of degradation parameters. We benchmark this framework against an MCMC baseline across various synthetic fouling and leakage scenarios, including challenging low-probability, sparse-event failures. The results show that SBI achieves comparable diagnostic accuracy and reliable uncertainty quantification, while accelerating inference time by a factor of82$\times$ compared to traditional sampling. The amortized nature of the neural network enables near-instantaneous inference, establishing SBI as a highly scalable, real-time alternative for probabilistic fault diagnosis and digital twin realization in complex engineering systems.

### [2] [The High Explosives and Affected Targets (HEAT) Dataset](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18828)
*Bryan Kaiser, Kyle Hickmann, Sharmistha Chakrabarti, Soumi De, Sourabh Pandit, David Schodt, Jesus Pulido, Divya Banesh, Christine Sweeney*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文发布了HEAT数据集，这是一个包含高爆炸药驱动冲击波在多种材料中传播的模拟数据的物理丰富数据集，旨在促进人工智能/机器学习模型在多材料冲击波传播领域的应用。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 目前缺乏公开可用的数据集用于训练、测试和验证机器学习模型，以模拟高爆炸药驱动的冲击波在多种材料中的动态过程。HEAT数据集的发布旨在填补这一空白。

Method: HEAT数据集由洛斯阿拉莫斯国家实验室开发的欧拉多材料冲击波传播代码生成，包含两种分区：膨胀冲击波圆柱体(CYL)模拟和扰动分层界面(PLI)模拟。数据集包含压力、密度、温度等时间序列数据。

Result: HEAT数据集捕捉了动量传递、冲击波传播、塑性变形和热效应等关键现象，提供了丰富的多物理场数据，包括固体、液体、气体和高爆炸药等多种材料。

Conclusion: HEAT数据集为开发人工智能/机器学习代理模型，模拟多材料冲击波传播提供了一个宝贵的基准，并有望减少对昂贵且危险的实际高爆炸药测试的依赖，并可能应用于创造性视频生成。

Abstract: Artificial Intelligence (AI) surrogate models provide a computationally efficient alternative to full-physics simulations, but no public datasets currently exist for training and validating models of high-explosive-driven, multi-material shock dynamics. Simulating shock propagation is challenging due to the need for material-specific equations of state (EOS) and models of plasticity, phase change, damage, fluid instabilities, and multi-material interactions. Explosive-driven shocks further require reactive material models to capture detonation physics. To address this gap, we introduce the High-Explosives and Affected Targets (HEAT) dataset, a physics-rich collection of two-dimensional, cylindrically symmetric simulations generated using an Eulerian multi-material shock-propagation code developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory. HEAT consists of two partitions: expanding shock-cylinder (CYL) simulations and Perturbed Layered Interface (PLI) simulations. Each entry includes time series of thermodynamic fields (pressure, density, temperature), kinematic fields (position, velocity), and continuum quantities such as stress. The CYL partition spans a range of materials, including metals (aluminum, copper, depleted uranium, stainless steel, tantalum), a polymer, water, gases (air, nitrogen), and a detonating material. The PLI partition explores varied geometries with fixed materials: copper, aluminum, stainless steel, polymer, and high explosive. HEAT captures key phenomena such as shock propagation, momentum transfer, plastic deformation, and thermal effects, providing a benchmark dataset for AI/ML models of multi-material shock physics.

### [3] [Surrogate Functionals for Machine-Learned Orbital-Free Density Functional Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20458)
*Roman Remme, Fred A. Hamprecht*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种新的机器学习方法，用于构建轨道自由密度泛函理论 (OF-DFT) 中的替代泛函，该方法无需像传统方法那样对整个能量表面进行精确拟合，而是专注于确保密度优化能够产生正确的基态密度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统的机器学习 OF-DFT 方法在学习能量表面和处理大规模系统时面临挑战，例如需要大量训练数据、表示形式不匹配以及昂贵的正交归一化步骤。本文旨在解决这些问题，并实现更快的计算速度和更好的可扩展性。

Method: 研究人员引入了“替代泛函”的概念，其定义不再是普遍地忠实于物理参考，而是通过在固定优化程序中产生正确的基态密度来实现。他们提出了一种梯度下降改进损失函数，并结合自适应采样方案，将学习集中在优化轨迹上。训练仅需要基态密度，无需能量或偏离基态的梯度。

Result: 在 QM9 和 QMugs 基准测试中，替代泛函在密度误差方面达到了与全监督机器学习 OF-DFT 相当或更好的水平，同时消除了先前工作所需的 O(N3) 正交归一化步骤，从而提高了更大系统的运行时间缩放。

Conclusion: 本文提出的替代泛函方法为机器学习 OF-DFT 提供了一种更有效、更可扩展的途径，有望加速电子结构计算，并使其能够应用于更大规模的系统。

Abstract: We introduce surrogate functionals: machine-learned energy functionals for orbital-free density functional theory (OF-DFT) which are defined not by universal fidelity to a physical reference, but merely by the requirement that density optimization with a fixed procedure yields the true ground-state density. Helpfully, training surrogate functionals requires only ground-state densities, no energies or gradients away from the ground state. We here propose a gradient-descent-improvement loss that guarantees exponential convergence of the density to the ground state, and combine it with an adaptive sampling scheme that concentrates learning around the optimization trajectories actually visited during inference. On the QM9 and QMugs benchmarks, surrogate functionals achieve density errors competitive with or improving upon the state of the art for fully supervised machine-learned OF-DFT, while eliminating the need for the $O(N^3)$ orthononormalization step required by prior work, yielding improved runtime scaling for larger systems.

### [4] [Mechanistic Interpretability Tool for AI Weather Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20467)
*Kirsten I. Tempest, Matthias Beylich, George C. Craig*

Main category: physics.ao-ph

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种开源工具，旨在通过机械可解释性方法理解人工智能天气模型（AI Weather Models）的内部运作，并初步应用于GraphCast模型分析了中纬度天气系统和特定湿度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 人工智能天气模型预测能力迅速提升，但其内部机制如同黑盒，缺乏透明度。为了建立对这些模型的信任，需要理解其预测是如何产生的，机械可解释性提供了一种可能的框架。

Method: 该工具组织了模型处理器内部的潜在表示，并允许用户进行初步分析，包括余弦相似度和主成分分析（PCA），以识别潜在与气象特征相关的潜在空间方向。工具被应用于GraphCast模型，进行案例研究。

Result: 初步案例研究表明，该工具能够识别出潜在通道的线性组合，这些组合似乎对应于可解释的气象特征，例如中纬度天气系统和特定湿度。

Conclusion: 该工具为理解人工智能天气模型提供了有价值的手段，有助于建立对这些模型的信任，并可能揭示模型学习到的、人类尚未理解的天气关联性。

Abstract: Artificial Intelligence (AI) weather models are improving rapidly, and their forecasts are already competitive with long-established traditional Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP). To build confidence in this new methodology, it is critical that we understand how these predictions are generated. This is a huge challenge as these AI weather models remain largely black boxes. In other areas of Machine Learning (ML), mechanistic interpretability has emerged as a framework for understanding ML predictions by analysing the building blocks responsible for them. Here we present an open-source, highly adaptable tool which incorporates concepts from mechanistic interpretability. The tool organises internal latent representations from the model processor and allows for initial analyses, including cosine similarity and Principal Component Analysis (PCA), enabling the user to identify directions in latent space potentially associated with meteorological features. Applying our tool to the graph neural network GraphCast, we present preliminary case studies for mid-latitude synoptic-scale waves and specific humidity. These demonstrate the tool's ability to identify linear combinations of latent channels that appear to correspond to interpretable features.

### [5] [Machine learning moment closure models for the radiative transfer equation IV: enforcing symmetrizable hyperbolicity in two dimensions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20143)
*Juntao Huang*

Main category: math.NA

TL;DR: 本文将机器学习 (ML) 矩封闭模型扩展到二维物理空间和二维角空间 (2D2V) 的辐射传输方程 (RTE)，并提出了一种保证可对称化超音速性的框架。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 直接模拟辐射传输方程 (RTE) 存在维度灾难问题，矩方法是降低计算成本的一种方法，但面临矩封闭问题。为了解决这个问题，本文旨在开发一种结构保持的机器学习矩封闭模型，确保其超音速性。

Method: 本文保留了经典P N模型的领先部分，仅修改最高阶的块行。利用P N模型的结构特性，引入了一种块对角对称化器，并推导了保证结果ML系统可对称化超音速性的代数条件。这些条件导致了一种自然参数化，使用对称正定矩阵和对称封闭块，这些块可以从数据中学习。

Result: 数值结果表明，所提出的框架优于经典P N模型，同时保持了超音速性。

Conclusion: 本文提供了一种结构保持的机器学习矩封闭模型框架，可以有效地解决辐射传输方程，并保证了数值计算的稳定性和物理合理性，为未来在复杂辐射传输问题中的应用奠定了基础。

Abstract: This is our fourth work in the series on machine learning (ML) moment closure models for the radiative transfer equation (RTE). In the first three papers of this series, we considered the RTE in slab geometry in 1D1V (i.e. one dimension in physical space and one dimension in angular space), and introduced a gradient-based ML moment closure [1], then enforced the hyperbolicity through a symmetrizer [2], or together with physical characteristic speeds by learning the eigenvalues of the Jacobian matrix [3].   Here, we extend our framework to the RTE in 2D2V (i.e. two dimensions in physical space and two dimensions in angular space). The main idea is to preserve the leading part of the classical $P_N$ model and modify only the highest-order block row. By analyzing the structural properties of the $P_N$ model, we show that its coefficient matrices are symmetric and admit a block-tridiagonal structure. Then we use this property to introduce a block-diagonal symmetrizer for the ML moment model and derive explicit algebraic conditions on the closure blocks which guarantee the symmetrizable hyperbolicity of the resulting ML system. These conditions lead to a natural parametrization of the closure in terms of a symmetric positive definite matrix together with symmetric closure blocks, which can be learned from data while automatically enforcing symmetrizable hyperbolicity by construction. The numerical results show that the proposed framework improves upon the classical $P_N$ model while maintaining hyperbolicity.

</details>
