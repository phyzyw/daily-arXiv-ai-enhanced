### [1] [Physics-based phenomenological characterization of cross-modal bias in multimodal models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20624)
*Hyeongmo Kim, Sohyun Kang, Yerin Choi, Seungyeon Ji, Junhyuk Woo, Hyunsuk Chung, Soyeon Caren Han, Kyungreem Han*

Main category: cs.AI

TL;DR: 本文研究了多模态大语言模型（MLLM）中的跨模态偏差问题，并提出了一种基于物理现象的解释方法，通过构建代理物理模型来分析Transformer动态，揭示了输入模态之间的强化关系。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 尽管MLLM在多模态理解、推理和生成方面取得了进展，但其复杂的交互动态可能导致难以察觉的系统性偏差，传统的embedding或表示层面的分析难以完全捕捉到这些偏差。

Method: 研究团队开发了一种基于物理的代理模型来描述Transformer动态（语义网络结构和自/交叉注意力），并通过扰动分析（以情感分类为例）和动态分析（以Lorenz混沌时间序列预测为例）来诊断MLLM中的跨模态偏差。

Result: 实验结果表明，即使在具有不同架构的MLLM中，多模态输入也可能强化模态主导地位，而非减轻它，这通过结构化的错误吸引子模式和动态分析得以体现。

Conclusion: 本文提出了一种新的、基于物理现象的视角来理解和解决MLLM中的算法公平性问题，为进一步研究多模态模型的偏差和鲁棒性提供了新的思路。

Abstract: The term 'algorithmic fairness' is used to evaluate whether AI models operate fairly in both comparative (where fairness is understood as formal equality, such as "treat like cases as like") and non-comparative (where unfairness arises from the model's inaccuracy, arbitrariness, or inscrutability) contexts. Recent advances in multimodal large language models (MLLMs) are breaking new ground in multimodal understanding, reasoning, and generation; however, we argue that inconspicuous distortions arising from complex multimodal interaction dynamics can lead to systematic bias. The purpose of this position paper is twofold: first, it is intended to acquaint AI researchers with phenomenological explainable approaches that rely on the physical entities that the machine experiences during training/inference, as opposed to the traditional cognitivist symbolic account or metaphysical approaches; second, it is to state that this phenomenological doctrine will be practically useful for tackling algorithmic fairness issues in MLLMs. We develop a surrogate physics-based model that describes transformer dynamics (i.e., semantic network structure and self-/cross-attention) to analyze the dynamics of cross-modal bias in MLLM, which are not fully captured by conventional embedding- or representation-level analyses. We support this position through multi-input diagnostic experiments: 1) perturbation-based analyses of emotion classification using Qwen2.5-Omni and Gemma 3n, and 2) dynamical analysis of Lorenz chaotic time-series prediction through the physical surrogate. Across two architecturally distinct MLLMs, we show that multimodal inputs can reinforce modality dominance rather than mitigate it, as revealed by structured error-attractor patterns under systematic label perturbation, complemented by dynamical analysis.

### [2] [Scale-PINN: Learning Efficient Physics-Informed Neural Networks Through Sequential Correction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.19475)
*Pao-Hsiung Chiu, Jian Cheng Wong, Chin Chun Ooi, Chang Wei, Yuchen Fan, Yew-Soon Ong*

Main category: cs.CE

TL;DR: Scale-PINN 是一种新的 PINN 学习策略，它将数值求解器中的迭代残差校正原理融入损失函数，显著提高了 PINN 的收敛速度和精度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有 PINN 方法在训练速度和精度上仍然落后于现代数值求解器，限制了其在科学和工程领域的广泛应用。需要一种能够结合数值方法严谨性和深度学习灵活性的方法。

Method: Scale-PINN 将迭代残差校正原理直接整合到 PINN 的损失函数中，模仿数值求解器的迭代过程。这种方法避免了复杂的自适应采样和损失加权，并适用于各种物理领域的 PDE 问题。

Result: Scale-PINN 在多个 PDE 问题上实现了前所未有的收敛速度，例如，在具有挑战性的流体动力学问题上，将最先进的 PINN 的训练时间从数小时缩短到 2 分钟以内，同时保持了更高的精度。

Conclusion: Scale-PINN 通过结合数值方法和深度学习的优势，标志着 PINN 在科学和工程领域实际应用方面的重要进步，为可扩展的、基于物理信息的学习提供了新的途径。

Abstract: Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) have emerged as a promising mesh-free paradigm for solving partial differential equations, yet adoption in science and engineering is limited by slow training and modest accuracy relative to modern numerical solvers. We introduce the Sequential Correction Algorithm for Learning Efficient PINN (Scale-PINN), a learning strategy that bridges modern physics-informed learning with numerical algorithms. Scale-PINN incorporates the iterative residual-correction principle, a cornerstone of numerical solvers, directly into the loss formulation, marking a paradigm shift in how PINN losses can be conceived and constructed. This integration enables Scale-PINN to achieve unprecedented convergence speed across PDE problems from different physics domain, including reducing training time on a challenging fluid-dynamics problem for state-of-the-art PINN from hours to sub-2 minutes while maintaining superior accuracy, and enabling application to representative problems in aerodynamics and urban science. By uniting the rigor of numerical methods with the flexibility of deep learning, Scale-PINN marks a significant leap toward the practical adoption of PINNs in science and engineering through scalable, physics-informed learning. Codes are available at https://github.com/chiuph/SCALE-PINN.

### [3] [Exact Discrete Stochastic Simulation with Deep-Learning-Scale Gradient Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.19775)
*Jose M. G. Vilar, Leonor Saiz*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种新的方法，将精确的离散随机模拟与深度学习规模的梯度优化相结合，克服了传统方法在参数推断和逆设计的维度瓶颈。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统的精确离散随机模拟（如Gillespie算法）由于其离散事件选择的非可微性，与基于梯度的优化技术不兼容，导致高维参数空间下的参数推断和模型设计受限。现有的梯度估计方法要么方差过大，要么计算成本过高，而软向前方法则牺牲了物理精确性。

Method: 该方法通过完全解耦前向模拟和反向微分来实现精确性和可微性。前向模拟使用标准的硬类别采样以确保物理精确性，反向微分通过连续大规模并行Gumbel-Softmax直通替代项进行传播。

Result: 该方法在多种模型上进行了验证，包括可逆二聚化模型、基因振荡器、203,796参数的基因调控网络（达到98.4%的MNIST准确率）和离子通道门控的实验性膜片钳记录，展现了高精度、可扩展性和可靠性。GPU实现速度达到每秒19亿步，与非可微模拟器相当。

Conclusion: 这项研究表明物理精确性和可微性并非相互排斥，为系统生物学、化学动力学、物理学等领域中精确随机模拟的大规模参数推断和逆设计开辟了新的可能性。

Abstract: Exact stochastic simulation of continuous-time Markov chains (CTMCs) is essential when discreteness and noise drive system behavior, but the hard categorical event selection in Gillespie-type algorithms blocks gradient-based learning. We eliminate this constraint by decoupling forward simulation from backward differentiation, with hard categorical sampling generating exact trajectories and gradients propagating through a continuous massively-parallel Gumbel-Softmax straight-through surrogate. Our approach enables accurate optimization at parameter scales over four orders of magnitude beyond existing simulators. We validate for accuracy, scalability, and reliability on a reversible dimerization model (0.09% error), a genetic oscillator (1.2% error), a 203,796-parameter gene regulatory network achieving 98.4% MNIST accuracy (a prototypical deep-learning multilayer perceptron benchmark), and experimental patch-clamp recordings of ion channel gating (R^2 = 0.987) in the single-channel regime. Our GPU implementation delivers 1.9 billion steps per second, matching the scale of non-differentiable simulators. By making exact stochastic simulation massively parallel and autodiff-compatible, our results enable high-dimensional parameter inference and inverse design across systems biology, chemical kinetics, physics, and related CTMC-governed domains.

</details>
